# Evaluation and calibration gates

## Gate A — deterministic conservation

- empty and malformed inputs fail safely;
- source IDs remain stable;
- numbers, quotations, citations and defined terms are indexed;
- exact duplicate candidates are found;
- weak transitive bridges do not create one unsafe family;
- protected evidence and structural recurrence are not removed;
- partial overlap is withheld;
- every exact transaction is scoped, reversible and hash-bound.

## Gate B — semantic adjudication

Create a labelled corpus spanning legal, technical, academic, business, creative
and personal writing. Every pair must receive independent labels for semantic
relation, local function, residual payload and safe disposition. Calibrate
thresholds by class and cost: false deletion is substantially more costly than a
missed consolidation.

Minimum adversarial categories:

- negation with high lexical similarity;
- changed number, date, entity or jurisdiction;
- qualification or exception appended to a repeated claim;
- evidence and conclusion stating similar content for different reasons;
- intentional formula repeated at methodological checkpoints;
- one bridge occurrence sharing different payloads with two families;
- pronoun or antecedent dependency outside the donor sentence;
- quotation, citation or defined term present in only one occurrence;
- repeated recommendation supported by distinct reasons;
- apparent paraphrase that reverses direction of causation.

## Gate C — donor and receiver rewrite

For every proposed transaction verify exact source-unit provenance, residual-payload
retention, grammatical repair, receiver completeness, cross-reference resolution
and mutation-scope isolation. Inject deliberately defective model proposals and
prove that Python rejects them.

## Gate D — global stabilisation

Compare each committed state with both the immediately preceding document and the
immutable original. Measure unresolved accidental burden, change spillover,
hard-anchor preservation, ledger reconciliation and family stability. Additional
passes require measurable expected benefit greater than conservation risk.

## Gate E — final certification

The final badge remains disabled until all critical invariants pass, every family
is committed, preserved or explicitly reviewed, no critical semantic discrepancy
is detected, and the complete document has passed global reread. Report confidence
and unresolved uncertainty; never translate a similarity score into “100% semantic
equivalence.”
